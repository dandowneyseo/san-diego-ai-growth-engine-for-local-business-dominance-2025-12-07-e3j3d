Cookies and Tracking Technologies

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit our website. They help us recognize you, remember your preferences, and understand how you use our Services. Cookies can be either "persistent" (remaining on your device until deleted) or "session" (deleted when you close your browser).

Types of Cookies We Use

Our Services use the following types of cookies:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the basic functionality of our Services
  • Performance Cookies: Help us understand how you use our Services and improve functionality
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences and settings
  • Marketing Cookies: Track your activity across websites to deliver targeted advertising
  • Analytics Cookies: Collect information about how you interact with our Services

Other Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we use other tracking technologies, including:

  • Pixels and Web Beacons: Small graphic images that track page visits and user behavior
  • Local Storage: Data stored locally on your device
  • Log Files: Server logs that record information about your interactions with our Services
  • Analytics Tools: Third-party services that track and analyze user behavior

Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through browser settings. You can typically:

  • Refuse to accept cookies
  • Delete existing cookies
  • Receive a warning before cookies are placed on your device

Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality and performance of our Services. For more information about managing cookies, visit www.allaboutcookies.org or www.youronlinechoices.com.
